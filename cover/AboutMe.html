I started my love for languages in a different area, studying Sign Language at GRCC, because I was working at a company called Alternative Pathways. Taking care of adults with mental and physical disabilities, I started learning because my main client happened to be deaf.
I fell away from that role when my family began to grow. So I went back to college to learn auto mechanics. I started a job at Borgman ford while I was still learning, I was hired as a Quick lane technician, I started showcasing my knowledge by asking to take on jobs that required more knowledge and understanding. While performing these jobs I was asked to transition over to the used car department. When I was at a loss, I would turn to the internet, as most people do. I would find myself, more often than not, finding the answers quickly but without the actual physical knowledge of how to perform the tasks, still taking longer than I would have liked on the jobs.
Realizing that maybe automotive wasn’t for me, I went back to school again. I went to a bootcamp for C# and found that I was in love with it. I learned quickly and gained rapport with the other students. I was enjoying myself everyday, even if the problems that I was solving were small, but frustrating. Come Final Project time, I remember Sweating profusely on a stage in front of about 100 or so people that had come just to see the project that my team and I were working on.
